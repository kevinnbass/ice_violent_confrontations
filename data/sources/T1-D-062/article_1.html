    Philippines citizen dies in ICE custody at Texas medical center

    HARLINGEN, Texas A 72-year-old convicted child sex offender and citizen of the Philippines died on December 5 at Valley Baptist Medical Center while in ICE custody. The man, Pete Sumalo Montejo, was pronounced dead at 2:20 p.m. by the emergency physician on duty.

    Montejo, who entered the United States as a lawful permanent resident in 1962, had a history of legal troubles, including a 1992 conviction for aggravated sexual assault of a child in Harris County, Texas. More recently, he was arrested in May 2022 for driving while intoxicated and failing to register as a sex offender, and again in July 2024 for possession of a controlled substance, according to the press release.

    ICE arrested Montejo on February 25 during a targeted enforcement action, and he was subsequently transported to the Montgomery Processing Center. He appeared before an immigration judge in March, but the case was reset. Montejo faced multiple hospitalizations between May and November 2025 for various naturally caused illnesses, including anemia and septic shock from pneumonia, before returning to ICE custody, stated the source.

    Following his death, ICE notified the Department of Homeland Securitys Office of Inspector General, ICEs Office of Professional Responsibility, and the Philippines consulate, as required by agency policy. ICE also makes official notifications to Congress, nongovernmental organization stakeholders, and the media upon an official report of a detained illegal aliens death, according to the press release.

    ICE emphasizes its commitment to providing comprehensive medical care to those in its custody, ensuring safe, secure, and humane environments. Reports of in-custody deaths are posted on the ICE website within two business days, with additional details made public within 90 days, in compliance with congressional requirements.
