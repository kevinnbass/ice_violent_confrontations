( June 2025 ) ( Learn how and when to remove this message ) June 2025 Los Angeles protests Part of 2025 United States protests against mass deportation and June 2025 Los Angeles County immigration raids Date June 6, 2025 - present Location Los Angeles County Goals Ending mass deportation Methods Rioting, Demonstrations , civil disobedience Parties Los Angeles Police Department , Immigration and Customs Enforcement , California National Guard Protesters Casualties and losses 3 injured 44 arrested The 2025 Los Angeles protests are an ongoing series of civil disturbances in the Los Angeles . Protests began in Los Angeles on June 6, when Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) arrested 44 people. &#91; 1 &#93; &#91; 2 &#93; On June 7, it was announced that the National Guard would be deployed to quell the protests. Timeline [ edit ] June 6 [ edit ] Protests began on June 6 when the ICE arrested 44 people. People protested mostly near the Home Depot in the Westlake District, while Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents in riot gear clashed with the protesters. ICE also arrested several people at a clothing store and clashed with activists. Later, the Los Angeles Police Department engaged in intense standoffs with protestors in which tear gas was used. &#91; 3 &#93; June 7 [ edit ] On June 7, protests occurred in Paramount . Protestors blocked a street with shopping carts and a recycling bin. Federal agents then deployed flash bangs and pepper balls, injuring two people. According to an attorney with the Coalition for Humane Immigrant Rights of Los Angeles , some protestors were throwing bricks and others looked injured. &#91; 4 &#93; In an interview with Fox News , border czar Tom Homan announced that he would send the National Guard into Los Angeles that night. &#91; 5 &#93; According to Governor Gavin Newsom , the federal government is going to take over the California National Guard and deploy 2,000 soldiers.
