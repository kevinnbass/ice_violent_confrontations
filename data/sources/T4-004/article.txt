( September 2025 ) ( Learn how and when to remove this message ) Shooting attack in Texas, U.S. 2025 Dallas ICE facility shooting Part of Deportation in the second Trump administration Location Love Field , Dallas, Texas , U.S. Coordinates 32°49′24.5″N 96°52′20.4″W &#xfeff; / &#xfeff; 32.823472°N 96.872333°W &#xfeff; / 32.823472; -96.872333 Date September 24, 2025 6:40 a.m. CDT Attack type Murder-suicide , sniper attack Weapons Sniper rifle Deaths 3 (including the perpetrator) Injured 1 On September 24, 2025, an individual shot three people detained by United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents at an immigration facility near Interstate 35E in the Love Field area of Dallas , Texas, before shooting and killing himself afterward. The shooter fired multiple rounds from a roof or an elevated position down into the field office's sally port . &#91; 1 &#93; One of the detainees shot was killed on scene, while the other two were taken to nearby hospitals, with one of them pronounced dead after arrival. All three victims were shot at a van in the sallyport . &#91; 2 &#93; &#91; 1 &#93; No federal agents were wounded or injured. &#91; 3 &#93; Shooting [ edit ] This section needs expansion . ( September 2025 ) Investigation [ edit ] In a Twitter post, FBI Director Kash Patel shared images of unspent shell casings with the words "ANTI-ICE" written on them, alleging that they belonged to the suspect. &#91; 4 &#93; Reactions [ edit ] Vice President JD Vance posted a statement on Twitter , saying that the "obsessive attack on law enforcement, particularly ICE, must stop". He added in a statement that he's "praying for everyone hurt in the attack and for their families." Secretary Kristi Noem also responded on a social media post. &#91; 5 &#93; United States Senator John Cornyn called the shooting "horrific" and said that "he would keep everyone involved in his prayers, and promising a full investigation." &#91; 6 &#93; Texas Senator Ted Cruz also weighed in, saying that his team "is closely monitoring the situation at the facility". He spoke that "his team are praying for the swift recovery of those injured, and we are deeply grateful to the brave first responders who rushed to the scene." &#91; 7 &#93; Marc Veasey also released a similar statement, saying that "his office is aware of the developing situation and will continue to monitor the situation", adding that "they're keeping the victims in prayer." &#91; 8 &#93; See also [ edit ] 2025 Alvarado ICE attack References [ edit ] ^ a b "Live updates: 2 dead in shooting at Dallas ICE facility; suspect is dead" .
