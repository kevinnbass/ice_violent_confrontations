(PHOTOS) More than 1,000 people protest ICE arrests in DeKalb County

Chamblee, GA During a Feb. 1 rally on Buford Highway, more than 1,000 people protested the recent wave of U.S. Immigrations and Customs Enforcement (ICE) arrests.

On Sunday, Jan. 26, U.S. Immigrations and Customs Enforcement agents made an unspecified number of arrests in Chamblee, Brookhaven, Doraville and Tucker. ICE has not responded to multiple requests from comments from Decaturish about the arrests, including the number of people arrested, where the arrests occurred and the specific charges against those arrested. One of those arrests reportedly occurred outside a Tucker church during a service.

The Feb. 1 rally lasted for hours as demonstrators held signs and waved flags in front of Plaza Fiesta in Chamblee and along Buford Highway.

The Party for Socialism and Liberation organized the rally to support the immigrant community.

Natalie Villasana, an organizer with the PSL, said, In the first week of the Trump administration, we have seen ICE raids here in Atlanta as well as Chicago, Denver-Aurora, Los Angeles, and more. ICE is arresting thousands and terrorizing our communities. Now is the time to send a clear message to the Trump administration: We will fight back.

Decaturish media partner Atlanta News First reported that four people were arrested during the protest. Two people were arrested for reckless driving, one person was arrested for obstruction and one person was arrested for disobeying an authorized person directing traffic, ANF reported.
